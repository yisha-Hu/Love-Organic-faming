frequently asked questions

what is kildare organic farm

The kildare farm is to integrate community and agriculture in a mixed, biodiverse, sustainable and environmentally responsible farm, managed for the benefit of everyone involved. kildare farm operate under the model of community supported agriculture(CSA). IN CSA there is a direct relationship between the farmers and those who consume the food produced.

How do the farm work

Kildare farm is a not-for-profit enteprise run on cooperative principles by voluntary and open membership. The farmers work the land and the consumers become members of the farm, committing to pay in advance a certain amount of money for an agreed period of time, in return, the members receive a box of seasonal vegetables each week during our nine month season. The member's money provides a modest income for the farmer and cover the day-to-day running cost of the farm.

How can I get involved in working on the farm

Volunteers are always needed and appreciated in kildare farm,. For anyone wishing to help with farm work, they may contact the farm or Volunteer coordenator directly-info@kildarefarm.ie. All volunteers must be over 18 and we can also accept vulnetable adults with additional needs if accompanied by a support person, as well as third-level work experience students.

How do the membership work

Membership of the farm involves sharing the risk of a poor harvest with the farmer and the other members. With the CSA model it is understood by all that we share equally in the risk and reward. the due date for signing up for each season is March. Subscription for the year must be received before then. You can also become a social member at a cost of 30 euro per household per year, you will be invited to all the farm events and will have an opportunity to participate in the running of the farm and become part of the farm community. you can also take part in volunteering in any way that suits you.
